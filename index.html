Operator's instincts, strategist's ambition.

I'm 24 and based in New York. I've worked supply chains from every altitude: warehouse floor, co-op, Big 4 intern, and now consultant on Fortune 50 enterprise transformations. The pattern: I learn a level, then move up one, and keep what the floor taught me.

My generation is entering operations just as AI forces the biggest rethink in thirty years. That's the opportunity of a career. The old instinct was to optimize what exists. The new question is whether it should exist at all. I'd rather be one of the people asking it than one of the people defending the old answer.
